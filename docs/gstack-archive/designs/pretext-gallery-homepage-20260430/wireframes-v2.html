What v2 changed vs v1

  • S1 home — added a top system bar (version/pack count/live status), multi-specimen stack (warm + caffeine + bubblegum simultaneously), washi tape, rubber-stamp in clay, 60-sticker callout sheet, handwritten annotation + hand-drawn arrow, a social-proof strip at bottom with real numbers, horizontal pack shelf with 4 richly-colored cards (not a grid of cards, but 4 deliberately different colored sheets with varied typography per pack).
  • S2 pack detail — preview now a stack (two sheets overlapping), washi tape on top, circular badge "130pp", breadcrumb bar with metadata chips, pull-quote from HN, 4 CTA buttons with 4px offset solid shadow (clicky, retro-terminal-product feel), metadata as colored chips, 12 actual mini-page-previews (not dot swatches) for the themes row — each shows a real-looking page in that theme.
  • S3 theme-swap — dark top banner with mode badge, large green success toast with render time, dashed-amber URL block showing the live shareable-URL, animated cursor overlay, specimen rotated on the preview, ring stamp on the specimen ("v5.2"), much bolder typography throughout.
  • S4 Codespaces — now has a proper paper-textured hero banner above the terminal, a progress ring clock (conic-gradient, 62% ready), 5 numbered tour steps with avatars and detail lines, a big "while you wait" tip box, and a terminal pane with mac window-chrome, timestamps, colored log lines, live progress bar, and a blinking cursor.

Weight sources (each earns its pixels)

  • Multiple real specimens on home — the product IS the art direction
  • Washi tape + rubber stamps + sticker sheets — physical artefact energy, not SaaS slickness
  • Handwritten annotations (Caveat + Reenie Beanie) — human in the loop
  • Bold shadows on CTAs — Swiss-adjacent, retro-terminal, click-feedback
  • Typography at display scale — 78px headline, 64px title, Fraunces 800
  • Real numbers in proof strip and chips — concrete not aspirational
  • Colored pack cards — each pack OWNS its own color, no sea of white
  • Pull-quote with attribution — social proof baked into the detail page
  • Meta as pills — readable, colored, scannable
